📖 How to Use QuizLock
▼
Before Your Class
1. Enter your name and email at the top of this page — this links all your tests to your account so only you see them in the saved list.
2. Prepare your Excel question file. Row 1 is a header row and is ignored. Data starts from row 2:
Col A — Question Col B — Option 1 Col C — Option 2 Col D — Option 3 Col E — Option 4 Col F — Correct
Your question here Option A Option B Option C Option D 2
Col F must be the number 1, 2, 3 or 4 — indicating which option is correct. Maximum 25 questions per test.
3. Fill in the test title, date, your email, and time limit. Edit the default instructions if needed.
4. Upload your Excel file and click Generate QR Code. The test is saved automatically.
In Class
1. Project the QR code on your classroom screen.
2. Ask students to enable Do Not Disturb on their phones before scanning.
3. Students scan the QR, enter their full name and university email, and press Begin.
4. The screen locks immediately. Switching apps, minimising, or pressing Home will auto-submit the test with marks obtained so far.
5. The timer counts down on screen. The test auto-submits when time runs out.
6. Students see a confirmation screen and receive their score and pass/fail by email automatically.
After Class
• Click 📧 Summary on any saved test to receive a full class report by email — class average, highest and lowest scores, and a list of any students auto-submitted for leaving the screen.
• If you teach the same test across multiple groups on different days, do not click Release Results yet. Students receive only their score after each session — not the correct answers — so there is nothing to share with the next group.
• Once all groups have sat the test, click 🔓 Release Results. Every student who submitted will receive a second email with the full list of correct answers. This cannot be undone.
Important Notes
• Results are saved in a Google Sheet called QuizLock Results in the Google Drive of the account that deployed the Apps Script. Each test gets its own tab named after the test title.
• Students cannot retake a test on the same device. If a student has a genuine technical issue, ask them to try a different browser or speak to you directly.
• If a student's submission fails due to a connection issue, the portal retries automatically. If it still fails, they will see a message asking them to show you their screen.
• The pass mark is 40%.
• Please coordinate with colleagues — do not run two tests at exactly the same time as all tutors share the same Google Sheet.
